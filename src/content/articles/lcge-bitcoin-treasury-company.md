Canada's Lifetime Capital Gains Exemption (LCGE) lets a Canadian-resident individual shelter a meaningful chunk of capital gain on the sale of shares in a **Qualified Small Business Corporation** — a tax-advantaged structure most Canadians never touch unless they're founders.

This piece works through a specific question: **can a personally-owned Bitcoin treasury company qualify as a QSBC?** What the "active business" test actually requires, how the asset-mix and use-of-funds rules treat a treasury balance sheet, where the CRA has drawn lines on similar holding structures, and the practical setup steps if it's viable. Not financial or legal advice — just thinking out loud about a structure most Canadian holders haven't considered.
