Tesla and SpaceX are the public-market and private-market sides of one unified bet on vertical integration across compute, manufacturing, energy storage, autonomy, and orbital infrastructure. Strategy lays out why they belong together in the framework.

The operational question this piece tackles: **how do you size the public side of a thesis whose private side you can't directly own?** SpaceX is the larger business by most measures of strategic optionality, but the only way in is through the Tesla share — which has to carry the weight of both. A look at how that asymmetry should — and shouldn't — affect position sizing on TSLA specifically.
