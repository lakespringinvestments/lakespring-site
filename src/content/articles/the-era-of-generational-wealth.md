The orthodoxy of personal finance — buy a low-cost index, dollar-cost average, hold for forty years — was forged in an economy where the underlying transformations were slow and broadly distributed. Indexing captured the average return of the market because the market itself was the transformation.

That era is ending. AI, sovereign-grade compute, and the digital-asset rails are concentrating value into a much smaller number of companies and assets, on a much shorter timescale. **The forty-year ETF return profile and the ten-year concentrated-thesis return profile are no longer comparable bets.** This piece walks through why generational wealth in this cycle looks less like compounding the average and more like owning the transformation.
