Malaysia's Foreign-Sourced Income (FSI) exemption is one of the most generous regimes in Southeast Asia for incoming residents — but the headline framing ("income earned abroad is tax-free") obscures the planning work that actually matters.

For a Canadian relocating to Kuala Lumpur with a portfolio still domiciled at home, the practical question is **which slice of investment return is actually foreign-sourced** under both the Malaysian rules and the Canada–Malaysia tax treaty. Where the source rules treat capital gains differently than dividends, where the Canadian departure-tax rules cut in, and the structuring choices that determine whether the FSI exemption is a genuine windfall or just an accounting illusion.
