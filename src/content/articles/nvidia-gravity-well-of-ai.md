The thesis page argues Nvidia sits at the gravitational center of the AI buildout — picks-and-shovels infrastructure, CUDA lock-in measured in a decade of developer mindshare, and execution cadence the field hasn't been able to match.

The harder question this piece tackles: **what would actually unseat them?** AMD's MI300 ramp, custom silicon from the hyperscalers, the emerging "AI ASIC" thesis, and the recurring "China has its own stack" story — a short list of credible threats and a sober read on why none of them are close enough to change the position size today.
